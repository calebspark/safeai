The Governance Imperative

Two Failure Modes.
One Solution.

Singapore's enterprises are moving from AI pilots to production deployment — but the governance question remains unanswered at the use-case level.

Under-Governed

AI Deployed Without Guardrails

Enterprises adopt AI in ways that expose customers, staff, and society to avoidable harm — without adequate technical controls or accountability. The risk is not in moving fast. The risk is in treating AI governance as something to retrofit once problems emerge.

Over-Governed

Innovation Paralysed by Compliance

Blanket compliance processes designed for high-stakes scenarios are applied uniformly to low-risk tools — creating friction that stalls innovation and drives AI initiatives underground, where they are governed even less effectively.

⚖️

SafeAI.sg is established to close this gap — Singapore's industry-led Centre of Excellence providing risk-weighted, use-case-specific governance that enables confident AI deployment at the appropriate level of rigour, for any organisation, at any scale.

Mission Delivery

Five Pillars.
Full Governance Lifecycle.

SafeAI.sg delivers its mission through five mutually reinforcing pillars — spanning use-case risk assessment, technology demonstration, workforce capability, agentic runtime governance, and AI cost oversight.

01
Free · Open · Self-Service

Open Risk Assessment Toolkit & UARS Standard

A practitioner-validated, self-service scoring toolkit freely accessible online — no registration required. Any Singapore organisation applies the Universal AI Use-Case Risk Score (UARS) to determine the right governance tier for any AI use case in under 30 minutes.

Outcome: Singapore enterprises gain immediate, actionable, cost-free access to calibrated AI governance guidance.
02
Demonstrate · Not Endorse

Technology Associate & Showcase Programme

Technology companies demonstrate how AI governance controls work in real production environments — through reference architectures, case studies, and community events. SafeAI.sg does not test, certify, or endorse any Associate technology.

Outcome: Singapore becomes a credible regional demonstration environment for enterprise AI governance, supporting EDB, JTC, and IMDA's positioning.
03
Level 0 → Level 3

Professional Certification & Skills Development

Co-developed with Singapore's institutes of higher learning — four certification levels from career conversion for non-technical professionals through to Chief Architect for enterprise governance leaders. Aligned to SkillsFuture frameworks and eligible for applicable skills funding.

Outcome: Singapore builds a sustainable, skilled AI governance workforce — ensuring responsible AI deployment is an organisational capability.
04
Intent Window Principle

Agentic Runtime Observability & Governance

Govern declared agent intent before action is taken — not after. SafeAI.sg addresses three critical gaps the industry has not yet resolved: pre-execution intent interception, real-time trajectory monitoring, and adversarial context integrity. The key insight is that agentic AI systems declare intent before they act — that window is where governance must operate.

Outcome: Singapore establishes a first-mover position in agentic AI governance — addressing risks that most enterprise security programmes have not yet incorporated.
05
Inform · Optimise · Operate

AI FinOps & Cost Governance

Token economics (the cost and consumption of AI compute units, i.e. "tokens") as a governance concern — from budget controls and context hygiene to denial-of-wallet (DoW) attack detection and AI cost anomaly as a security signal. Responsible AI governance extends beyond safety and compliance: as AI workloads scale, unmanaged token spend creates material exposure.

Outcome: Organisations gain the controls to treat AI cost as a shared governance responsibility — not just a billing line item.
