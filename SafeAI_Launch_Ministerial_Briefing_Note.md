## 2. Context and Significance

### What is SafeAI.sg?

SafeAI.sg is Singapore's **industry-led Centre of Excellence for AI technical safety and practical governance**, operationally anchored at the Punggol Digital District with institutional support from JTC Corporation, IMDA, and CSA. It is established and operated by SPARK by CIO Academy Asia — Singapore's senior technology leadership community representing approximately **16,000 CIOs, CTOs, and technology practitioners** across the region.

SafeAI.sg bridges the gap between Singapore's national AI governance policy frameworks and practitioner-level implementation realities. It is a non-regulatory, independent knowledge centre providing:

- Risk-weighted, use-case-specific guidance
- An open governance controls toolkit
- A professional certification pathway
- A trusted community of practice

### Why This Launch Matters

The SafeAI.sg launch coincides with Singapore's position at the forefront of global AI governance. As a co-convener of the **International AI Safety Report** — the largest global scientific collaboration on AI safety, backed by over 30 countries — Singapore has established itself as a credible voice in responsible AI deployment. SafeAI.sg translates that international standing into practical, on-the-ground infrastructure.

## 3. SafeAI.sg Charter Objectives — Three-Point Brief

SafeAI.sg delivers its mission through three mutually reinforcing pillars spanning the full lifecycle of AI governance readiness.

### Pillar 1 — Open Risk Assessment Toolkit and Governance Controls Standard

SafeAI.sg will develop and freely publish the **Universal AI Use-Case Risk Score (UARS)** — a self-service, five-dimension scoring instrument that any Singapore organisation can apply to any AI deployment to determine the appropriate tier of governance required. Alongside the UARS, SafeAI.sg publishes a full governance controls toolkit specifying commensurate controls, implementation processes, and a policy-as-code extensibility framework.

**Outcome:** Singapore enterprises and public agencies gain immediate, actionable, cost-free access to calibrated AI governance guidance — reducing both under-governance risk and innovation-stalling over-governance.

### Pillar 2 — Technology Showcase and Associate Programme

SafeAI.sg operates an **Associate Programme** for AI platform providers, cybersecurity vendors, observability tooling companies, and governance technology firms to demonstrate how AI governance controls can be embedded into real-world enterprise deployments. Associates showcase through community events, reference architecture demonstrations, and case study publications.

**Outcome:** Singapore becomes a credible regional demonstration environment for enterprise AI governance — supporting EDB, JTC, and IMDA's positioning of Singapore as the regional hub for trusted, responsibly deployed AI.

### Pillar 3 — Professional Certification and Skills Development

SafeAI.sg will co-develop a structured **professional certification programme** in partnership with Singapore's institutes of higher learning, spanning four levels — from career conversion for non-technical professionals through to senior certification for enterprise architects and AI governance leaders.

**Outcome:** Singapore builds a sustainable, skilled AI governance workforce — ensuring responsible AI deployment is an organisational capability, not a compliance exercise.

## 8. Suggested Talking Points for SMS Tan Kiat How

1. **AI Safety Is a Foundation, Not an Afterthought** — Every organisation embarking on an AI journey should establish its governance foundation at the outset. The cost of building AI safely from the start is a fraction of the cost of remediating ungoverned AI systems after the fact.

2. **The AI Landscape Evolves Faster Than Any Single Organisation Can Track — Collaboration Is the Only Sustainable Answer** — AI risk is not static. SafeAI.sg provides the structured channel for industry-regulator exchange to happen continuously, not just at point-in-time consultations.

3. **Governance Frameworks Only Have Value If They Can Be Operationalised** — SafeAI.sg closes the last-mile gap: translating written governance principles into implemented technical controls. Its open toolkit bridges policy and practice.

4. **Risk-Based Governance Is the Only Approach That Scales** — Not all AI carries the same risk. The UARS framework enables proportionate governance: lightweight controls for low-risk applications, rigorous safety stacks for high-stakes deployments.

5. **Industry-Led, Government-Anchored: Singapore's Model for Trusted AI** — The Advisory Council assembled at the launch — spanning CSA, IMDA, JTC, CSIT, MDDI, AI Singapore, the Singapore AI Safety Institute, Temasek, DBS, Grab, and Google — is a working governance body, not a symbolic gesture.
