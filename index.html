The Industry Challenge

Singapore's enterprises are moving from AI pilots to production, but the governance question remains unanswered at the use-case level.

Under-governed

AI Deployed Without Guardrails

Enterprises adopt AI in ways that expose customers, staff, and society to avoidable harm, without adequate technical controls or accountability. The risk is not in moving fast. It is in treating AI governance as something to retrofit once problems emerge.

Over-governed

Innovation Paralysed by Compliance

Blanket compliance processes built for high-stakes scenarios get applied uniformly to low-risk tools. The friction stalls innovation and drives AI initiatives underground, where they are governed even less effectively.

Managing the Delicate Governance Balance

SafeAI.sg is established to close this gap. Singapore's industry-led Centre of Excellence provides risk-weighted, use-case-specific governance that enables confident AI deployment at the appropriate level of rigour, for any organisation, at any scale.

Our Mission

Five Pillars. Full-Stack Governance.

SafeAI.sg delivers through five mutually reinforcing pillars, spanning risk assessment, technology demonstration, workforce capability, agentic runtime governance, and AI cost oversight.

01
Free, open, self-service

Open Risk Assessment Toolkit and Governance Standard

Singapore enterprises gain immediate, actionable, cost-free access to calibrated AI governance guidance.
02
Demonstrate, not endorse

Technology Associate and Showcase Programme

Singapore becomes a credible regional demonstration environment for enterprise AI governance, supporting MNCs and attracting leading AI companies to leverage the Singapore ecosystem for global growth.
03
Level 0 to Level 3

Professional Certification and Skills Development

Singapore builds a sustainable, skilled AI governance workforce, making responsible AI deployment an organisational capability.
04
Intent window principle

Agentic Runtime Observability and Governance

Singapore takes a first-mover position in agentic AI governance, addressing risks most enterprise security programmes have not yet incorporated.
05
Inform, optimise, operate

AI FinOps and Cost Governance

Organisations gain the controls to treat AI cost as a shared governance responsibility, not just a billing line item.
